# Set Up X/Twitter

Link your X/Twitter account so your agent can post tweets, write threads, and interact with content on your behalf.

## What you'll need

- An X/Twitter account
- Access to your Klaws dashboard

## Step-by-step setup

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Channels">
    Open your agent's dashboard and find the **Channels** section. Select **X/Twitter**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Connect your account">
    Click **Connect** and log in with your X/Twitter account. You'll be asked to authorize Klaws to post on your behalf.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Approve permissions">
    Review the permissions and approve. Your agent will be able to read and post tweets on your account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test it out">
    Go to the web chat and ask your agent to draft a tweet. Once you're happy with it, ask it to post.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What your agent can do on X/Twitter

- **Write and post tweets** — Ask it to create and publish a tweet
- **Write threads** — Create multi-tweet threads on any topic
- **Search X** — Find tweets, trends, and conversations about a topic
- **Schedule posts** — Combine with [scheduled tasks](/features/tasks) to post at set times

<Tip>
  Combine X/Twitter with scheduled tasks to build an autopilot content strategy. For example, schedule a task that finds trending topics in your niche and drafts a tweet every morning.
</Tip>

<Warning>
  Always review what your agent posts, especially when automating. You're responsible for what goes out on your account.
</Warning>

## Common questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can my agent reply to other people's tweets?">
    Your agent can search and read tweets. Posting replies may be supported depending on your skills and setup.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Will my agent post without asking me?">
    Only if you set up a scheduled task that tells it to post automatically. In regular chat, your agent will draft content and wait for your approval before posting.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Can I disconnect my account?">
    Yes. Go to Channels in your dashboard and remove the X/Twitter connection at any time.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
